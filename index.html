Selected works
Deep reinforcement learning for conjugate heat transfer

I was recently involved in a project with E. Hachem, P. Meliga and H. Ghraieb concerned with the use of DRL for the passive control of heat transfer problems.

DRL for passive flow control

I am co-supervising the PhD of H. Ghraieb, who worked on passive flow control using single-step deep reinforcement learning. See related publication here.

Drag prediction using CNN

I recently experimented with the training of a CNN on a dataset of arbitrary shapes to learn to predict drag coefficients at low Reynolds number. The code and datasets are open-source. See related publication here and here.

Diogenes

Between 2015 and 2018, I have been leading the development of the Diogenes software suite. It is based on discontinuous Galerkin methods, and is devoted to the solving of nanophotonics problems in time- and frequency-domain. It includes several advanced features, such as curvilinear cells, multi-element meshes, high-order polynomial expansions and others.

PBC with oblique incidence

I worked on the extension of the field transform technique to handle polychromatic plane wave analysis at oblique incidence with periodic boundary conditions in time-domain. Here, a doubly periodic transmittive color filter illuminated from below with a 30 degrees incidence angle. See related publication here.

Polychromatic pulses in waveguides

I developed a specific source module able to take into account mode dispersion (index and field profile) to propagate polychromatic pulses in waveguides. The mode solution and the dispersion relation can be analytic, or provided through external files. Here, a TE01-to-TE02 mode converter is simulated on a 2 GHz large spectrum. See related publication here.

Visualization module

I wrote a visualization module to post-process 3D results from the Diogenes DGTD solver. This feature provides a sampling of the solution over a given plane by projecting it on quadrilaterals. The discontinuity at material interfaces is respected, and the geometry is fitted with a recursive cutting approach.

Curvilinear elements in a nanosphere dimer

A part of my PhD was dedicated to the study of isoparametric elements in nanophotonics computations (see here). Here, we show the resonance appearing in the gap between two metallic nanospheres discretized with quadratic tetrahedra.

Shape optimization using deep reinforcement learning

I have worked on the generation of 2D aerodynamic shapes from scratch using a deep reinforcement learning algorithm. See related publication here.

Field prediction using U-nets

I am co-supervising the PhD of J. Chen, with whom we worked on the training of U-nets using a dataset of arbitrary shapes to learn to predict velocity and pressure fields at low Reynolds number. See related publication here.

Strip waveguide analysis

I added tools in the Diogenes suite for the analysis of waveguides, such as time-domain sources computed from 2D frequency-domain field maps, or mode overlap computation.

RCS computations

I added a module for the computation of Radar Cross Sections in the Diogenes suite. It was succesfully validated on a blazed grating simulation, with an almost perfect 2.7 degrees deflection from the normal. The RCS is given in squared micrometers.

Multi-element meshes

The basis library of the Diogenes software suite is able to handle multi-element meshes composed of both hexahedra and tetrahedra (reading, neighbor-finding, partitionning, ...). Here, a hybrid mesh processed for the Diogenes-HDGFD solver.

Textured photovoltaic cells

We worked on the meshing and computation of realistically-textured photovoltaic cells from scattered plot data with Alexis Gobe. This is part of a collaboration between INRIA and the FZJ.

Dielectric metasurface

During my PhD, I made a short numerical study on the effects of lithography defects on the behavior of reflectarrays (see here). Here, a non-cartesian doubly-periodic anisotropic metasurface is used to deflect a normally-incident plane wave with a non-cartesian angle.
